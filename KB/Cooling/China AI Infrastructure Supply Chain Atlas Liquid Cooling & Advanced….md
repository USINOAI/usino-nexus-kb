**July 2026 | USINO.AI Intelligence Layer**  
  
## Executive Overview  
**USINO.AI VIEW:** China's AI computing infrastructure supply chain is bifurcating into two critical operational layers: thermal management (liquid cooling) and packaging architecture (glass substrates, chiplets). As of July 2026, the domestic liquid cooling market reaches approximately RMB 24 billion with >40% annual growth, while semiconductor packaging—particularly glass-based substrates and advanced interconnects—represents the next-generation bottleneck shaping US-China technological competition. This brief maps the institutional supply chain across both verticals, identifying white-list qualified suppliers, financing profiles, geopolitical exposure, and financial risk surfaces.  
**Market Scale:**  
* Liquid cooling server market: ~RMB 24 billion (2026E)  
* Thermal management growth rate: >40% YoY  
* Advanced packaging valuation peak: +200% cumulative gains since early 2026  
* Top-four liquid cooling leaders: >70% combined market share  
  
## Part I: Liquid Cooling Supply Chain Architecture  
Liquid cooling technology has shifted from emerging niche to operational imperative for AI data centers. The supply chain divides into three distinct segments: systems integration (cold plates, immersion tanks), component distribution (CDUs, manifolds, quick disconnects), and thermal interface materials (TIM).

## Part II: Advanced Semiconductor Packaging — Glass Substrate & Chiplet Architecture  
As liquid cooling resolves thermal constraints within server architecture, the packaging substrate layer becomes the next limiting factor for AI chip density and performance. The emergence of chiplet architectures, HBM (high bandwidth memory) stacking, and glass-based substrate technologies fundamentally reshape the supply chain from traditional wire-bonded organic substrates toward glass interposers and precision interconnect systems.  
## Packaging Architecture Overview  
**Current State (July 2026):**  
* Chiplet + advanced packaging adoption now mandatory for leading-edge AI chips (NVIDIA, AMD, Intel validation pipelines)  
* Glass substrate technology transitioning from research to early production phase  
* Chinese domestic suppliers pursuing substitution across multiple substrate types  
* Valuation multiples at historical highs; sector accumulated >200% gains since early 2026  
**Supply Chain Sequence:**  
1. Glass substrate material + processing (Caihong, BOE, Wego)  
2. Through-glass-via (TGV) and precision interconnect (Wego, domestic OSAT fabs)  
3. Chiplet integration + testing (SJ Semiconductor, JCET, TFME)  
4. Advanced packaging materials (EMC, photoresist, underfill compounds)  
  
## Segment 2.1: Glass Substrate Manufacturing  
Glass substrates represent the next-generation substrate architecture for chiplet integration, HBM stacking, and chiplet-on-package (CoPoS) configurations. Unlike traditional organic substrates, glass offers superior thermal conductivity, dimensional stability at scale, and native through-glass-via (TGV) capability.
